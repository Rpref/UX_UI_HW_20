PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Ben’s Bells is a local nonprofit with highly recognizable murals around town, yet many people are unaware of their mission and what they do.

We set out to discover how to increase their visibility in the community, thereby increasing engagement and donations.

USER RESEARCH

To get started, we first needed an understanding of who our user might be. We created a proto persona to target who we could interview in order to gather more information. We interviewed 5 people, aged 40-66 who have children and live in the Tucson or Phoenix area.

Our goal was to gain insight on how people have interacted with charitable organizations.

How & when they have donated either time or money?

What makes them trust an organization or want to get involved?

What do they know about Ben’s Bells specifically?

INSIGHTS

We synthesized our interview data using affinity mapping, and were able to identify some common themes.

We discovered that interviewees:

Became involved with an organization through people in their community.

Were more likely to donate if they had interacted with the organization.

Recognized Ben’s Bells mosaics around town but didn’t know much about the organization.

IDEATION

Now that we understood who our user was, we could go deeper into thinking about what they might want out of an experience with the website.

We developed a feature prioritization matrix from the research to determine which areas needed to be the focus.

Two main areas stood out: the kindness calendar and donation form and process.

STORYBOARD

Next, we created a storyboard to walk us through how our user might come to the site and how they might feel after using the site. This gave us more clarity on our featured functions and what purpose they might serve.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

The last thing we needed to do before creating our prototypes was a competitor analysis. We wanted to see what other organizations in this space were doing, and how they were presenting their information and engaging their users.

Our main takeaways from this exercise were to add clear and mutliple “Donate” buttons to the Ben’s Bells site, and to reorganize the information so that it looked clean, modern, and was interactive.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Our research and ideation work revealed clearly what our problem was that needed solving:

People need to trust an organization, or otherwise be familiar with it, in order to donate or get involved; and they get involved through word of mouth from people in their communities.

We determined that we might be able to help if we can make users feel a more personal connection to the people involved in Ben's Bells, as well as understand the direct impact of their contributions.

We might do this by prominently showcasing the founder and her story, displaying more photos of volunteers, making the Kindness Calendar interactive, and providing clarity on where donation funds are allocated.

USER TESTING

Now that we had our prototypes, we wanted to see how users would experience engaging with our key features.

To collect qualitative data, we conducted 2 rounds of usability testing, consisting of 3 user tests for each round.

ROUND 1

Users were tasked with completing 3 tasks on the mobile wireframe.

ROUND 2

A different set of users were tasked with completing the same 3 tasks on the desktop wireframe.

TASKS

1. Sign up for the newsletter.

2. Share your participation in the Kindness Calendar via Facebook.

3. Go on the website and make a donation.

TEST RESULTS

Testing gave us a lot of insight into things that we needed to change about the prototype in order to make the experience run more smoothly.

We took the insights from our testing and put them into a prioritization matrix in order to determine which updates were the most impactful at this time, keeping in mind the user’s needs as well as the needs of the organization.

FINAL PRODUCT | COMPARISON

Through interviews, competitive analysis, and user testing, we had identified the main areas to focus our efforts to increase engagement with the Ben’s Bells website. Through our redesign of the website, we:

  • Brought the founder’s story front and center in order to connect the user to the mission of the organization.
  • Included more visuals and a short desciption of what the organization does in order to provide clarity to the user.
  • Added more ways to donate.
  • Made the Kindness Calendar interactive and sharable so as to increase community engagement with the organization, and visibility into it’s mission.
